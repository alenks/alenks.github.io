About Me

I am a doctoral candidate at the School of Computing, National University of Singapore (NUS) advised by Prof. Trevor E. Carlson. My research interests lie broadly in the area of computer architecture, program analysis, and distributed systems. With the end of Dennard scaling and Moore's law, computer architecture research is undergoing a radical shift towards domain-specific architectures. This resulted in an unprecedented need to codesign hardware and software. In my research career, I aim to design energy-efficient, optimized computing methodologies and architectures making use of ML techniques that help reduce the global carbon footprint. I currently work on speeding up the simulation of general-purpose synchronizing multithreaded applications on heterogeneous hardware by intelligently choosing parts of the workload that needs to be analyzed to determine the overall behavior.

Besides academics, I like to spend free time reading (poetry, philosophy, Malayalam literature). I hail from Kerala, and hold a strong political stand. I am interested in photography and a sports enthusiast.
